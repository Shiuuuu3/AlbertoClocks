1. Casio G-Shock watches have unbeatable durability and rigidity


Casio is currently one of the largest watch manufacturers in Japan after Seiko and Citizen and it can be said that the product line that makes Casio's name globally is the G-Shock line of watches. G-Shock watches were developed by engineer Kikuo Ibe with the Casio team in 1983 and as of 2017, G-Shock watches have sold over 100 million units and are among the best selling watches of all time.

The reason Kikuo Ibe created the G-Shock line of watches is because Kikuo Ibe accidentally dropped and broke a precious watch his father gave him on one of his trips to work, so Kikuo Ibe determined to make the best shock resistant watch ever. And through the process of research and invention, the first G-Shock watch was born, bearing the code G-Shock DW5000c was born, with the meaning that G-Shock is Gravity Shock - meaning the ability to resist shock force from gravity up to 10m, and the manufacturing process of this watch follows a G-shaped table.

Regarding the design to increase the shock resistance of the watch, Kikuo Ibe was inspired by the structure of the ball result in football, Kikuo Ibe realized that the core of the ball is connected to the ball case so that no matter what Whatever the external force is not affected, Kikuo Ibe then applied this design principle to G-Shock watches, the movements of G-Shock watches are not attached to the cases but always have The gaps and the machine part are connected to the housing by a small plastic connecting rod to minimize the external force on the machine part.


The design structure inspired by the soccer ball makes G-Shock watches sturdy and the efficiency of every G-Shock watch produced has to go through. The very tough endurance testing process set forth by Kikuo Ibe includes being subjected to gravity shock, 10 bar water resistance, being able to withstand hot and cold temperatures and withstanding the force of heavy objects. large rolled over. Currently, the Casio brand that holds the Guinness World Record has a watch that is able to withstand a car with a load of nearly 25 tons and still works well through the Casio G-Shock DW5600E-1 model.

2. Water resistance to 10 bar or more

All current G-Shock models are water resistant to at least 10 bar or more, each G-Shock watch shipped from the factory must be water pressure tested in an underwater environment of 100m.


In addition, in the G-Shock collection there is a line dedicated to diving, the Frogman line, which has achieved ISO 6425 international standard of diving watches with frogman water resistance of 20 bar or more, and functions for people. specializes in scuba diving such as directional compass, automatic depth measurement when diving, moon phase announcement and tide level.

3. Long lifespan

The standard of use of each G-Shock watch must be anti-static from a height of 10m, water resistance of 10 bar and a battery life of 10 years, meaning that after 10 years of use, another battery must be replaced. , and the price to replace the G-Shock battery is quite cheap.

The 10-year battery life is an estimate only, if you waste the functions and the led the G-Shock will wear out in about 10 years or a little less. Notice how the G-Shock's battery life is 11 years if you rarely use the watch's functions.
